Rishti — Design Narrative

Designing for touch, pause and feeling

Rishti is a studio practice that traces how materials and mechanisms make people feel. We design with sensitivity — composing objects and moments that hold attention, invite care, and communicate intent through touch.

Practice note
Tactile probes, visible mechanisms, and calibrated resistance.

Values

Emotional Clarity

Design that surfaces affect—objects that make feelings legible through rhythm and material.

Material Honesty

Choose materials for their sensory truth and social meaning; let behavior be seen and felt.

Mechanism as Narrative

Mechanical action tells a story—visible actuation becomes an invitation rather than a mystery.

Material + Mechanism

We work at the scale of the hand: surface, give, resist and pause. Mechanisms are simplified to communicate intent; materials are tuned to invite touch.

Friction & Give
Tuning tactile responses to guide pacing.
Visible Linkages
Mechanisms that communicate cause and effect.

Interaction Study

A probe: the background and a small swatch respond to presence and movement. Move your mouse—notice how the light and wash subtly follow your hand, suggesting material directionality.

Touch
This single interactive affordance (mouse movement) modulates the perceived orientation and warmth of the material field—useful to explore how attention is directed by micro-cues.
